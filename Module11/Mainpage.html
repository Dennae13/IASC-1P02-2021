Paris, France





General History


Paris’s history dates to approximately 259 BC when the Celtic tribe Parisii settled on the banks of Seine (History of Paris - A Revolutionary History, n.d.). The Romans took over the fishermen village and called that Gallo-Roman town Lutetia in 52 BC (History of Paris - A Revolutionary History, n.d.). The city’s name was changed to Paris during the 4th century. In this period...

  • Attila the Hun threatened the city with his army.
  • The people of Paris were able to withstand the attacks due to Saint Geneviève’s intervention.

There was prosperity throughout the 11th century due to trading silver and it was a strategic route for pilgrims and traders. (History of Paris - A Revolutionary History, n.d.).



In the 12th century, the Notre Dame Cathedral began to be built, this project continued for about 200 years (Nozio, n.d.). The first university in France was also founded at the beginning of the twelfth century due to uprisings from students and professors. (History of Paris - A Revolutionary History, n.d.). Robert do Sorbon was appointed by the chaplain Louis IX to establish the College, it was eventually named ‘the Sorbonne’ after him. (History of Paris - A Revolutionary History, n.d.).
